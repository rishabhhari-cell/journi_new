## Design Context

### Users
Clinician-researchers and academic authors — from early-career to late-stage scientists. They are domain experts but **not necessarily tech-savvy**. They're juggling clinical duties, research, and administrative overhead around manuscript submission. Their context: frustrated by fragmented tooling (Word, email threads, journal portals), time-poor, and looking for something that just works. The job to be done: go from draft manuscript to published paper with minimal friction — choose the right journal, format correctly, collaborate with co-authors, submit, track, and resubmit.

### Brand Personality
**Calm. Simple. Efficient.**

- Voice: confident but never loud. Academic credibility without stuffiness.
- Tone: direct, supportive, understated. The product should feel like a trusted colleague who handles the tedious parts silently.
- Emotional goal: users should feel **excited and impressed** on first contact — recognising a high-quality, professional product — then quickly settle into a sense of relief and flow as they work.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Nordic Academic** — minimalist without sterility, Scandinavian calm with academic gravitas.

**References (and what to take from each):**
- **Heidi Health** — clinician-focused product design; clear workflows tuned for medical professionals, trustworthy feel
- **Asana** — productive, high-throughput workflow UI; clear task states, satisfying progression, never overwhelming
- **Notion** — simple, modular, efficient interface; blocks-based mental model, generous whitespace, understated chrome

**Anti-references:**
- Cluttered dashboards with competing widgets and dense information walls
- "AI-generated" generic SaaS aesthetic — cookie-cutter gradients, stock illustrations, predictable layouts
- Disjointed multi-tool experiences — the flow from dashboard to editor to submission must feel seamless and continuous, not like stitched-together pages

**Theme:** Light mode only. Cream and off-white warmth where appropriate (hero shader), snow-white for working surfaces.

### Design Principles

1. **Flow over features** — Every screen should guide the user toward the next natural step. The journey from dashboard to submission should feel like one continuous experience, not disconnected pages. Minimise cognitive switches.

2. **Calm confidence** — Use whitespace generously. Let elements breathe. Avoid visual noise, competing accents, or unnecessary decoration. When something is important, let it stand alone rather than shouting.

3. **Invisible efficiency** — The best interactions are the ones users don't notice. Auto-formatting, smart defaults, inline feedback. Remove steps wherever possible. The tool should feel like it's doing work *for* you.

4. **Accessible by default** — Users range from digital-native postdocs to senior clinicians who rarely leave Word. Target WCAG AA. Prioritise clear labels, obvious affordances, readable type sizes, keyboard navigation, and reduced-motion support. Never assume technical fluency.

5. **Authentic craft** — Avoid anything that looks templated or auto-generated. Every detail — spacing, transitions, micro-copy — should feel intentional and human. Quality over quantity in visual elements.

### Signature Elements

- **Frosted-glass navbar** — `backdrop-blur(12px) saturate(180%)` with 80% white background
- **Fade-up entrance animations** — staggered 90ms delay, 500ms duration, custom cubic-bezier
- **Hero mesh gradient** — subtle cream/sage MeshGradient at 40% opacity, 0.15 speed
- **Green progress lines** — thin animated lines for transitions and loading states
- **Card hover** — border shifts to `journi-green/40` with soft green shadow (`journi-green/5`)
